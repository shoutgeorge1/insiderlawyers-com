Stage 3 and Stage 4 Bed Sores

Pressure ulcers (bed sores) develop when sustained pressure cuts off blood flow—often in residents who can’t reposition themselves. Stage 3 involves full-thickness skin loss and may expose fat; Stage 4 can expose muscle, tendon, or bone and carries the highest risk of infection and serious harm. These advanced stages are often strong evidence of neglect when a facility failed to provide proper repositioning and wound care.
