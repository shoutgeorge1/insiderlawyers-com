How Adjusters Value (or Devalue) Injury Claims

Insider Accident Lawyers is a Los Angeles personal injury firm with former insurance defense experience. We understand how adjusters assess claims, which factors increase value, and what documentation strengthens negotiations. This guide explains the core valuation elements and how to avoid common pitfalls.

Key Takeaways

  • Adjusters evaluate coverage, liability, injury severity, and treatment history.
  • Gaps in care or weak records reduce claim value.
  • Objective evidence and consistent treatment improve outcomes.
  • Trial-ready preparation changes negotiation dynamics.
  • Our team includes litigators with decades of trial experience and a record that highlights 100 million+ recovered across 5,000+ cases.
"Adjusters Manipulate the Data" — Shawn S. Rokni, Insider Lawyer Personal Injury Playbook

Adjusters often start with a low reserve and raise authority in small steps. A strong liability narrative and clean medical timeline help move those internal approvals faster.

Adjusters weigh credibility, prior claims, and social media activity. Clear, consistent documentation helps neutralize those objections.

Adjusters often reduce claim value when treatment is delayed or inconsistent, so documentation matters.

Why Experience Matters

Former insurance defense insight helps counter valuation tactics.

  • We prepare records to show causation and long-term impact.
  • We position the claim for litigation if undervalued.

  • Coordinate medical records and treatment summaries

  • Build a demand package that aligns liability and damages

FAQs

These answers summarize common questions based on the Playbook and standard California practice.

What does an adjuster look at first?

Adjusters start with liability clarity, objective medical proof (imaging, specialist notes), treatment consistency, and coverage limits. We build a clean timeline to remove those objections.

Do treatment gaps hurt my claim?

Yes. Adjusters treat gaps as proof an injury was not serious. If there is a delay, we document the reason and keep treatment consistent.

How do I prove pain and suffering?

Proof usually includes medical imaging, specialist evaluations, and records that connect the injury to the incident, plus witness or scene evidence.

Will my case go to court?

Most cases settle, but trial-ready preparation improves leverage and protects value if litigation is required.
