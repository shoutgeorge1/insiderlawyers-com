Liability and Legal Framework

Comparative Negligence in Private Property Crashes

Parking lot cases frequently involve reciprocal duty arguments: lookout, speed control, and safe backing. California comparative negligence means insurers can reduce value with even modest fault assignments unless lane-right and movement priority are well documented.

Burden of Proof and Private Property Context

A private property accident lawyer claim must establish movement sequence in spaces with inconsistent markings and low witness reliability. We focus on lane rights, turning radius, stopping opportunities, and vehicle damage orientation.

Evidence Preservation: Surveillance and Maintenance

Private lot cameras are often overwritten quickly. We pursue preservation requests immediately and evaluate property records where design defects or poor visibility contribute.

Insurance Tactics

Common tactics include "mutual fault," "minimal impact means minimal injury," and "no clear right-of-way." Structured evidence and treatment chronology neutralize those tactics.

What Builds a Strong Parking Lot Case

  • Scene photos and lane-layout documentation
  • Camera footage requests to property owner/manager
  • Witness contact capture and statements
  • Vehicle damage orientation analysis
  • Medical records tied to crash mechanics
  • Parking garage condition and lighting records where relevant

FAQs

Who has right of way in parking lots?

It depends on lane flow, backing status, and local lot markings.

Are these cases always small?

No. Injury severity and liability complexity can be substantial.

Can I recover if I was partially at fault?

Yes, with value reduced by your comparative fault percentage.

What evidence helps most?

Surveillance, scene geometry, impact mapping, and coherent treatment records.

Do property records matter?

Yes, especially when lot design or maintenance contributed.
