Nursing Home Neglect vs Elder Abuse

Google loves definition pages. Here’s the difference between neglect and abuse—and why it matters for your case.

What Is Nursing Home Neglect?

Neglect is the failure to provide the care a resident needs, resulting in harm. It is usually not intentional. Examples: failing to turn a resident (causing bed sores), not providing enough food or water, ignoring infections, or not assisting with mobility so the resident falls. The facility or staff failed to act when they had a duty to care.

What Is Elder Abuse?

Elder abuse typically involves intentional harm or mistreatment—physical abuse (hitting, restraining improperly), emotional abuse (humiliation, threats), sexual abuse, or financial exploitation. In California, the Elder Abuse and Dependent Adult Civil Protection Act covers both abuse and neglect and can allow for enhanced remedies (e.g., attorney fees, punitive damages) when conduct is reckless or malicious.

Why the Distinction Matters

Some cases involve only neglect; others involve both neglect and abuse. The legal theories and available damages can differ. A Los Angeles nursing home neglect lawyer or elder abuse attorney can review your facts and advise which claims apply.
