Do You Have a Nursing Home Neglect Case?

Short checklist:

  • Your loved one developed bed sores in a nursing home
  • The wound progressed to Stage 3 or Stage 4
  • Staff ignored infections or declining health
  • Hospital treatment became necessary

If these occurred, the facility may be liable.

Signs of Nursing Home Neglect

Watch for these warning signs. A nursing home neglect lawyer can help if you see them.

  • Bed sores or pressure ulcers
  • Unexplained bruising
  • Repeated falls
  • Dehydration
  • Malnutrition
  • Untreated infections
  • Medication errors
  • Poor hygiene

Many cases stem from understaffing. We investigate facility care and hold responsible parties accountable.

Pressure Ulcers and Bed Sore Negligence

Pressure ulcers develop when residents are not repositioned regularly. Poor blood flow leads to skin breakdown, deep wounds, and infection.

Four stages: Stage 1 (redness, skin intact) → Stage 2 (partial-thickness loss) → Stage 3 and Stage 4 (full-thickness loss; may expose tissue or bone). Stage 3 and Stage 4 ulcers are often strong evidence of neglect.

Learn more: Pressure ulcers and neglect, repositioning standards.

Why Nursing Home Neglect Happens

  • Understaffing — Too few staff to turn residents, answer calls, or give basic care. See understaffing lawsuits.
  • Poor training — Staff untrained in wound care, fall prevention, or spotting decline.
  • Failure to reposition — Direct cause of pressure ulcers and bed sores.
  • Inadequate wound care — Ignoring or undertreating wounds so they worsen.
  • Failure to monitor — Not tracking weight, hydration, infections, or condition changes.

Compensation in Nursing Home Neglect Cases

Families may recover damages for harm caused by neglect.

  • Medical expenses (hospital stays, wound care, surgery)
  • Pain and suffering
  • Rehabilitation costs
  • Wrongful death damages
  • Loss of companionship

Severe bed sore cases, infections, and wrongful death can support substantial recoveries. A Los Angeles nursing home neglect or wrongful death lawyer can evaluate your case.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as nursing home neglect?

Failure to provide needed care that results in harm—for example, not turning residents (causing bed sores), inadequate food or water, ignored infections, medication errors, or preventable falls. When that failure causes injury, a nursing home neglect lawyer can help you pursue a claim. See neglect vs. abuse for the distinction.

Can a nursing home be sued for bed sores?

Yes. If your loved one developed bed sores because the facility failed to provide adequate repositioning, wound care, or monitoring, you may have a negligence claim. A bed sore lawsuit can hold the facility accountable. We review records and advise.

How do pressure ulcers develop?

Prolonged pressure on the skin cuts off blood flow. Immobile residents must be repositioned regularly. When staff do not, or skip proper support and wound care, ulcers form and can progress through four stages. What causes bed sores and Stage 3 and 4 are often evidence of neglect.

How long do I have to file in California?

Generally two years from the injury or from when you knew (or should have known) of the harm. Wrongful death also has a two-year deadline. A lawyer can confirm the deadline for your case.
