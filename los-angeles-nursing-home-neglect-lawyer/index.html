Signs of Nursing Home Neglect

Watch for these warning signs of neglect. If you see them, a nursing home neglect lawyer can help.

  • Bed sores or pressure ulcers
  • Unexplained bruising or injuries
  • Sudden weight loss or malnutrition
  • Dehydration
  • Repeated falls
  • Poor hygiene
  • Untreated infections
  • Medication errors

Many cases stem from understaffing—not enough staff to turn residents, monitor wounds, or ensure nutrition and hydration. An Los Angeles personal injury lawyer with elder abuse experience can investigate.

Pressure Ulcers and Bed Sores

Pressure ulcers (bed sores) develop when sustained pressure cuts off blood flow—often in residents who can’t reposition themselves. They’re a serious sign of neglect.

Four stages:

  • Stage 1 — Redness or discoloration; skin intact.
  • Stage 2 — Partial-thickness loss; blister or shallow open area.
  • Stage 3 — Full-thickness loss; may expose fat.
  • Stage 4 — Exposed muscle, tendon, or bone; highest risk of infection and death.

Stage 3 and 4 ulcers are often strong evidence of neglect. Proper repositioning and wound care prevent or catch most bed sores early. When a facility lets wounds reach advanced stages, a pressure ulcer lawyer or bed sore lawyer can hold them accountable. Severe cases can lead to infection, sepsis, or wrongful death.

Why Nursing Home Negligence Happens

Common causes of nursing home negligence:

  • Understaffing — Too few staff to turn residents, answer calls, or give basic care.
  • Poor training — Staff untrained in wound care, fall prevention, or spotting decline.
  • Failure to reposition — Direct cause of pressure ulcers and bed sores.
  • Inadequate wound care — Ignoring or undertreating wounds so they worsen.
  • Failure to monitor — Not tracking weight, hydration, infections, or condition changes.

Many facilities put profits before safety. An elder abuse lawyer can investigate whether the facility’s choices caused your loved one’s injuries.

Compensation in Nursing Home Neglect Cases

Families may recover damages for harm caused by neglect.

  • Medical expenses (hospital stays, wound care, surgery)
  • Pain and suffering
  • Rehabilitation costs
  • Wrongful death damages
  • Loss of companionship

Severe cases—advanced bed sores, sepsis, or death—can support substantial recovery. A nursing home neglect lawyer can evaluate your case. For death claims, see our Los Angeles wrongful death lawyer page.

Who Can File a Claim

In California, these parties may bring a claim:

  • The injured resident (if competent)
  • A legal guardian
  • Family members (under elder abuse laws)
  • Estate representatives (wrongful death)

A nursing home neglect lawyer can advise who has standing and next steps.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as nursing home neglect?

Failure to provide needed care, resulting in harm—e.g., not turning residents (causing bed sores), inadequate food or water, ignored infections, medication errors, or preventable falls. When that failure causes injury, a nursing home neglect lawyer can help you pursue a claim.

How do pressure ulcers develop?

Prolonged pressure on the skin cuts off blood flow. Immobile residents must be repositioned regularly. When staff don’t, or skip proper support and wound care, ulcers form and can progress through four stages. Stage 3 and 4 are often evidence of neglect.

Can I sue a nursing home for bed sores?

Yes. If your loved one developed bed sores because the facility failed to provide adequate repositioning, wound care, or monitoring, you may have a negligence claim. A bed sore lawyer can review the records and advise.

How long do I have to file in California?

Generally two years from the injury or from when you knew (or should have known) of the harm. Wrongful death also has a two-year deadline. A lawyer can confirm the deadline for your case.
