Insider Case Evaluation Framework

Our auto accident strategy starts with the same four pillars we use in high-value litigation.

1) Liability

  • Fault proof from scene evidence, witness statements, and report analysis.

2) Impact

  • Collision force and mechanics tied to injury plausibility and causation.

3) Injuries

  • Immediate and delayed symptoms documented in a coherent treatment timeline.

4) Coverage

  • Primary, excess, and UM/UIM policy sources reviewed early to prevent missed value.
  • Early work in these four areas reduces delay tactics and improves settlement positioning.

FAQs

These are common questions after a crash.

What should I do immediately after a car accident in Los Angeles?

Get to safety, call 911 if needed, exchange info without admitting fault, take photos, get witnesses, and seek medical care even for delayed symptoms.

How long do I have to file a claim?

Most California personal injury claims have a two-year deadline, but government claims and certain malpractice cases can be shorter.

Will my case go to court?

Most cases settle, but trial-ready preparation improves leverage and protects value if litigation is required.

What if the other driver is uninsured?

Uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage may apply, and we investigate all available policies.

How much is a car accident case worth?

Value depends on liability strength, injury severity, treatment costs, wage loss, and available coverage.
