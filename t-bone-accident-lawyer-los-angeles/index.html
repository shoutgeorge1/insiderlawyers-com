Liability and Legal Framework

California Right-of-Way and Comparative Fault

Most t bone accident lawyer los angeles files begin with right-of-way analysis, then shift into comparative fault arguments. California pure comparative negligence permits recovery even when fault is shared, but every assigned percentage reduces value. Defense carriers often allege late yellow entry, unsafe turn execution, or speed contribution to drive down settlement authority.

Burden of Proof in Intersection Crashes

A strong intersection accident lawyer los angeles case must prove sequence: who had lawful movement rights, where each vehicle was at entry, and whether evasive opportunities existed. Jury-facing clarity matters at pre-litigation stage because adjusters evaluate trial risk before they increase reserves.

Evidence Preservation Priorities

Signal timing logs, business camera footage, and witness memory degrade quickly. We secure footage requests early, collect scene geometry, and document impact points before repairs erase structural evidence. In side impact crash injury claim files, vehicle intrusion photos are especially important for causation disputes.

Insurance Blame-Shifting Tactics

Common tactics include "both drivers entered on yellow," "line-of-sight obstruction," and "avoidable impact." We counter with traffic engineering context, map-based path analysis, and medical-mechanism correlation. A disciplined proof package prevents lowball fault splits from becoming the default valuation baseline.

FAQs

Who is usually at fault in a T-bone crash?

Usually the driver who failed right-of-way, but insurers often dispute timing and movement legality. Evidence decides.

Can I still recover if I share fault?

Yes, under California comparative negligence, but recovery is reduced by your fault percentage.

Why do insurers fight side-impact claims hard?

Exposure is often high due to serious injury patterns, so liability and causation are frequently contested.

What makes a strong intersection claim?

Signal evidence, witness consistency, impact mapping, and coherent medical causation records.

How fast should I act after an intersection crash?

Immediately. Camera and scene evidence can disappear quickly.
