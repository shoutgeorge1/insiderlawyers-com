Liability and Legal Framework

Comparative Fault and Missing Evidence

When evidence disappears, insurers fill gaps with claimant-fault assumptions. Comparative negligence allocations often rise when objective proof is missing.

Burden of Proof Depends on Preservation Discipline

A claim with preserved evidence can prove sequence, speed context, and mechanism. A claim without it often collapses into contested narratives.

Legal Hold and Spoliation Strategy

A legal hold letter accident notice can alert parties to preserve relevant records. In high-risk files, spoliation letter california strategy helps document preservation expectations and potential consequences.

Insurance Tactics

Carriers may rely on incomplete records to deny liability or reduce value. Early preservation neutralizes this by locking objective proof before positions harden.

What to Preserve First

  • Scene photos and video from all angles
  • Witness contacts and statements
  • Police and dispatch references
  • Nearby business/residential surveillance
  • Vehicle damage and black-box availability
  • Medical intake records and symptom timeline

FAQs

What is evidence preservation?

Securing key proof before it is lost, overwritten, or altered.

When should legal hold letters be sent?

As early as possible, especially when third-party records are at risk.

How fast does surveillance disappear?

Often within days, depending on system retention policy.

What is spoliation?

Loss or destruction of relevant evidence that may trigger legal consequences.

Does preservation improve settlement value?

Yes. It strengthens liability certainty and reduces denial leverage.
