Liability and Legal Framework

Comparative Fault and High-Exposure Claims

In high-value TBI files, even modest comparative fault allocations can materially reduce recovery. Liability proof quality remains essential.

Burden of Proof and Neuro-Causation

TBI accident compensation requires coherent linkage between crash event and neurological outcome. Clinical consistency across providers is critical.

Evidence Preservation and Specialist Timing

Early neurological documentation, symptom diaries, and testing referrals can significantly affect claim credibility and value trajectory.

Insurance Tactics

Carriers often argue symptom subjectivity, preexisting cognitive baseline ambiguity, and limited objective findings. We counter with integrated expert records and functional evidence.

What Builds a Strong TBI Case

  • Mechanism-of-injury crash documentation
  • ER and follow-up neurological records
  • Neuroimaging and specialist interpretation
  • Neuropsychological testing results
  • Work, academic, and daily function impact evidence
  • Future care and economic expert support

FAQs

How much is a TBI settlement worth?

It varies by evidence strength, severity, long-term impact, and liability posture.

Does imaging always show TBI?

Not always, but when present it can strengthen causation and severity arguments.

Is concussion automatically a low-value claim?

No. Functional impairment and documented progression drive valuation.

Can future care be recovered?

Yes, when medically and economically supported.

Why are TBI claims contested?

High exposure and complex symptom profiles create aggressive defense behavior.
