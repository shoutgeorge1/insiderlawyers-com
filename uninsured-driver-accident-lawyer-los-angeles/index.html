Liability and Legal Framework for UM/UIM Cases

Comparative Fault Still Reduces Recoveries

California comparative fault applies regardless of whether payment comes from a third-party carrier or UM/UIM coverage. If your insurer assigns 30% fault, your claim value can drop by 30%. We challenge unsupported fault percentages with scene analysis, witness statements, and consistency testing of impact narratives.

Burden of Proof in First-Party Adversarial Claims

In a UM/UIM case, policyholder status does not remove your burden. You still must prove negligence, causation, and damages. Many policyholders are surprised when their own carrier disputes treatment intensity or future care projections. Trial-ready documentation prevents your file from being treated as "soft."

Evidence Preservation and Coverage Preservation

Two types of evidence matter here: collision evidence and contract evidence. Collision evidence proves fault and injury. Contract evidence proves coverage trigger and compliance. We collect declarations pages, endorsements, and correspondence logs while simultaneously developing liability proof.

Insurance Tactics Specific to UM/UIM

Common tactics include delay under "coverage investigation," broad medical record fishing to dilute causation, offset inflation, and low reserve posture based on missing records. We counter by forcing issue-specific responses, clarifying policy language, and presenting damages in a mediation- or arbitration-ready format.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the other driver had no insurance?

You may still recover through UM coverage, depending on your policy and proof. Other parties or policies may also be available in some cases.

Is UM the same as UIM?

No. UM usually applies to uninsured or unidentified drivers; UIM usually applies when the at-fault coverage is insufficient for full damages.

Can my own insurer fight my claim?

Yes. First-party claims can still involve adversarial evaluation on fault, causation, and value.

Do I need a lawsuit to resolve a UM/UIM claim?

Not always. Some claims resolve in negotiation; others require arbitration or litigation posture to reach fair authority.

How soon should I start legal review?

Immediately. UM/UIM claims combine legal deadlines and contract requirements that can arise quickly after a collision.
