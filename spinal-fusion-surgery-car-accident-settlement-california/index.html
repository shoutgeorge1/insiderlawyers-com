Liability and Legal Framework

Comparative Fault and High-Damage Exposure

Because potential damages are substantial, comparative fault disputes are often aggressive. Liability proof remains essential even in medically severe files.

Burden of Proof on Surgery Necessity

Spinal surgery accident lawsuit value depends on demonstrating why surgery was reasonable and medically indicated, especially after conservative options were exhausted.

Evidence Preservation and Medical Continuity

Imaging chronology, specialist records, and treatment progression should be preserved in sequence to prevent defense narratives of unrelated degeneration.

Insurance Tactics

Insurers often claim surgery was elective, premature, or unrelated. Counterstrategy requires surgeon rationale, objective progression records, and functional evidence.

What Builds a Strong Fusion Claim

  • Collision severity and mechanism documentation
  • Pre-op and post-op imaging comparison
  • Conservative-treatment history before surgery
  • Surgeon and specialist necessity opinions
  • Work restrictions and earnings impact records
  • Future care and impairment support
